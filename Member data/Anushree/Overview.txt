                                                                             EXERCISES
To manage diabetes effectively, it's essential to incorporate a mix of exercises into your routine. Here are some beneficial options:

Aerobic Exercises

- Walking: A simple and effective exercise that improves insulin sensitivity and cardiovascular health. Aim for at least 30 minutes a day.
- Cycling: A low-impact exercise that's easy on the knees and great for cardiovascular health. 
- Swimming: A full-body workout that's gentle on joints and excellent for cardiovascular health. It's also stress-reducing and social.
- Dancing: A fun way to get a cardiovascular workout and manage blood sugar levels. Choose a style that suits you, like Zumba or ballroom dancing.
- Jogging/Running: Excellent cardiovascular workouts that improve heart health and reduce the risk of heart-related complications.

Strength Training Exercises

- Resistance Band Exercises: Great for building muscle strength and improving insulin sensitivity. Start with light resistance and gradually increase.
- Weightlifting: Helps build muscle mass, which improves insulin sensitivity and glucose control. Begin with light weights and progress gradually.
- Bodyweight Exercises: Squats, lunges, push-ups, and pull-ups are excellent for building strength and improving insulin sensitivity.

Flexibility and Balance Exercises

- Yoga: Improves flexibility, balance, and strength while reducing stress. 
- Pilates: Enhances core strength, flexibility, and body control. It can help improve insulin sensitivity and reduce blood sugar levels.


Tips

- Consult your doctor: Before starting any new exercise routine, consult with your doctor to determine the best exercises for your specific needs.
- Monitor blood sugar levels: Check your blood sugar levels before, during, and after exercising to understand how your body responds.
- Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after exercise to prevent dehydration.
- Aim for consistency: Exercise regularly, Aiming for 30-50min per day .
